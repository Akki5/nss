Evironment Cell:

Environment Cell: is a new cell but earlier different programs had been done by NSS pertaining to it.

  • Polythene Free Campus: *We did survey in the market, carried out awareness drives, jute & paper bags were made from the village women and sold them to the fruit vendors. Though polythene is banned in Roorkee with strict orders, It remains to be seen whether polybags are still in use in the campus. So we would like to know what do you think should be the action plan in this field? Should we put up posters and do active campaigning or not?
  • Setting of organic waste disposal unit* either in the form of a Gobar Gas Plant or encouraging organic composting. The organic waste composting is already being done in the campus and Prof Randhawa of Biotech Dept is available to guide and assist us in this respect. About the Gobar Gas plant we already have written a proposal and we need to implement. Prof Randhawa told that he personally does in situ organic composting in the campus.
  • Setting up dual dustbins (degradable and non degradable)* throughout the campus as we are severely short of dustbins in the campus. We plan to take up this activity as soon as the session starts.
  • Also yoga camps are held twice a year, to spread the concept of physical fitness and hygiene.
  • We also organize health check up camps as well as hold rallies etc to spread more insight on healthy lifestyle to the rural people living in villages near roorkee.

Human Welfare

  • Blood donation Camp*: A benchmark activity carried out twice an year by this cell(in association with Health Cell) is the Blood Donation Camp. It witnessed a collection of 150 units of blood the 1st year, which increased to 272 and 293 the year next and the last camp collected a whooping 525 units of blood.
  • Cloth Collection & Distribution*: Cloth collection and distribution activity was carried out twice in the previous year to provide clothing especially during winters for construction workers at various sites in IITR. We hope to discontinue this though, with the construction almost nearing end this summer.
  • Awareness campaign to save water and reduce the wastage of food in the mess*: Posters and flexes would be put up in the messes and near the taps to discourage the wastage of food and water in the mess. A survey regarding this has already been carried out in the previous semester in all the messes of IITR.
  • Guest Lectures & Workshops:We plan to call few eminent personalities from the field of social work and business to encourage the spirit of community service amongst us. Workshops in association with NGOs and organizations for effective teaching methods and ways to influence the society in general towards the cause of humanity are planned.
